# Why Staying Connected to Your Roots Actually Matters

"Why should I care about traditions my grandparents followed?"

It's a question millions of young Indians ask — especially those living in metros or abroad, navigating careers, relationships, and identities that look nothing like their parents' lives. And it's a fair question.

But the answer isn't sentimental. It's scientific.

## Identity Isn't Optional

Psychologists have a term for it: *cultural identity clarity* — the degree to which you understand and feel connected to your cultural background. Research consistently shows that people with higher cultural identity clarity report better mental health, stronger resilience under stress, and a more stable sense of self.

A 2019 study in the *Journal of Cross-Cultural Psychology* found that bicultural individuals (people navigating two cultures) who maintained strong ties to their heritage culture showed lower rates of anxiety and depression compared to those who fully assimilated or felt lost between cultures.

In other words: knowing where you come from isn't nostalgic — it's protective.

## The Diaspora Disconnect

For the estimated 32 million Indians living outside India, the challenge is acute. You grow up eating dal and rice but explaining it at school. You hear Sanskrit shlokas at home but can't explain what they mean. You celebrate Diwali but struggle to articulate *why* beyond "it's a festival of lights."

Over time, this creates a gap. Not a dramatic rupture, but a quiet erosion. You stop asking questions. The rituals become performative. The wisdom behind them fades.

And then one day, you feel unanchored — successful by every external measure, but missing something you can't name.

## Roots Aren't Chains

Here's what gets lost in the conversation: connecting to your roots doesn't mean living your grandparents' life. It doesn't mean accepting everything uncritically or performing rituals you don't understand.

It means understanding the *why* behind the practices. It means knowing that:

- **Pranayama** (yogic breathing) wasn't invented for wellness influencers — it's a 3,000-year-old system for regulating the autonomic nervous system, and modern neuroscience confirms it works.
- **The Bhagavad Gita** isn't a religious text demanding obedience — it's a philosophical dialogue about duty, choice, and meaning that rivals anything in Western existentialism.
- **Punya** (meritorious action) isn't about cosmic reward points — it's a framework for building pro-social habits rooted in empathy.
- **Panchang** (the Hindu almanac) isn't superstition — it's an astronomical system that tracked planetary movements millennia before telescopes existed.

When you understand the reasoning, the practices stop feeling like obligations and start feeling like tools.

## The Modern Bridge

This is what myDharma is building. Not a return to the past, but a bridge — from ancient knowledge systems to modern understanding. Every teaching comes with the *why*. Every practice is explained through science, history, and personal relevance.

You don't have to believe anything on faith. You just have to be curious.

Because the wisdom of your ancestors isn't a burden to carry. It's a foundation to build on.

And the first step is simply choosing to understand it.
