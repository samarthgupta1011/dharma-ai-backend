# Welcome to myDharma

The Bhagavad Gita was composed over two thousand years ago, yet its insights into anxiety, purpose, and self-knowledge remain strikingly relevant. Pranayama — yogic breathing — was practiced for centuries before modern neuroscience confirmed that slow, rhythmic breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system and measurably reduces cortisol levels.

So why does ancient Hindu wisdom still feel out of reach for so many people?

## The Gap We See

Most spiritual apps today fall into two camps. Western mindfulness apps like Headspace and Calm offer polished experiences but strip away the cultural roots that give practices their depth. On the other side, traditional Hindu apps dump hundreds of mantras and rituals without explaining *why* any of it matters — or how it connects to your actual life.

Neither approach works for the curious, modern mind that wants to understand before it believes.

## What We're Building

myDharma sits in the middle. We take authentic Hindu teachings — Gita verses, pranayama, acts of kindness (punya), and guided reflection — and present them with clarity, science, and personal relevance.

Tell us how you're feeling, and our AI curates a personalised journey through these four pillars. Every recommendation comes with the *why*: the neuroscience behind the breathing exercise, the philosophical context of the verse, the psychology behind the act of kindness.

## Our Philosophy

We believe wisdom should explain itself. No dogma, no guilt, no gatekeeping. If a practice resonates, embrace it. If it doesn't, move on. The ancient rishis were scientists of the inner world — and their discoveries deserve to be understood, not just followed on faith.

myDharma is for skeptics, seekers, and everyone in between. We're excited to share this journey with you.
